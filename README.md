**Bright Coffee Shop Analysis Case Study**
**Introduction**

The Bright Coffee Shop Analysis is a comprehensive, data-driven case study designed to deliver actionable insights into sales performance, customer behavior, and operational efficiency across multiple coffee shop locations. This project demonstrates the application of advanced analytical techniques to transform raw transactional data into meaningful business intelligence.
The analysis showcases the ability to combine data querying, visualization, and business interpretation to drive strategic decisions and optimize operational outcomes.

**Objective**

The project aims to:

-Analyze sales performance across multiple stores and time periods
-Identify top-performing products and categories
-Understand customer purchasing patterns by time of day
-Evaluate revenue distribution and trends across locations
-Generate data-driven recommendations to enhance profitability and operational efficiency

**Tools Utilized**

Miro: Structured the analysis using mind maps and question frameworks
Canva: Designed professional, recruiter-friendly visual presentations
Databricks (SQL): Executed data querying, transformation, and advanced analysis
Microsoft Excel: Conducted data cleaning, preprocessing, and exploratory analysis
Microsoft PowerPoint: Created polished presentations to communicate insights effectively

**📊 Project Overview**

This case study explores transactional data from Bright Coffee Shop stores, including:

Transaction IDs and timestamps
Product categories and names
Sales revenue
Store locations

The analysis focuses on:

1. Revenue Analysis
Revenue segmentation by product and store
Monthly and weekly revenue trends
2. Product Performance
Top 10 products by revenue contribution
Product category performance
Product frequency by store
3. Customer Behavior Analysis
Purchase distribution by time of day
Peak sales periods and traffic patterns
Insights into daily and weekly customer trends
4. Store-Level Insights
Comparative performance across locations
Store-specific operational observations and opportunities

**Methodology**
1. Data Understanding
Examined dataset structure, column types, and relationships
2. Data Cleaning
Removed inconsistencies and duplicates
Handled missing and anomalous values
Standardized data formats for analysis
3. Data Transformation
Created derived variables such as:
Time-of-day segments (morning, afternoon, evening)
Aggregated revenue metrics
Sales frequency calculations
4. Data Analysis (SQL)
Applied SQL queries for:
Aggregation and segmentation of revenue
Ranking products and stores
Trend identification
5. Visualization
Developed charts, dashboards, and insights-ready visuals using Excel and Canva
6. Insight Generation
Translated analysis into actionable business recommendations
Identified opportunities for operational improvement and revenue growth

**📈 Key Insights**
Mid-morning consistently represents the peak sales period across all stores
Certain products consistently outperform others and drive a significant portion of revenue
Revenue distribution varies by location, highlighting opportunities for tailored strategies
Some product categories have minimal impact on overall revenue and may require reevaluation

**💡 Strategic Recommendations**
Optimize staffing and inventory management for peak hours
Focus marketing and promotions on high-performing products
Revise underperforming product categories or consider phasing them out
Implement store-specific strategies based on local performance metrics

This case study demonstrates proficiency in data analytics, SQL, Excel, and visualization to deliver actionable insights. The findings offer Bright Coffee Shop practical strategies to optimize operations, enhance customer experience, and increase profitability.
